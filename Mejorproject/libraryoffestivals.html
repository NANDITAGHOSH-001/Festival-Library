FESTIVALS OF INDIA

India’s festivals are a vibrant mosaic of spiritual, seasonal, and cultural celebrations that bring communities together through color, light, music, dance, and feasting. From Holi—a jubilant springtime splash of colors symbolizing the victory of good over evil and renewal—to Diwali, the luminous Festival of Lights honoring prosperity and family, and Durga Puja/Dussehra, which dramatizes the triumph of the divine feminine, each festival reflects deep-rooted beliefs and shared values. Add to that region-specific celebrations like Pongal, Onam, and Bihu that honor the harvest and nature’s bounty—and you see how these traditions echo India’s diversity and unity in harmony.
